01 · The Problem

Great products die at launch — not because they're bad, but because their makers can't market them fast, well, or cheaply.

Builders ship, but the marketing — copy, images, announcement, positioning — is slow, scattered across a dozen tools, and usually needs hiring and coordinating several freelancers.

02 · What it does

A pure composer.

Praeco never does the specialist work itself. Like a general contractor, it discovers, hires, and pays specialist agents on the CROO marketplace, quality-checks them, and composes the results into a finished launch kit.

You talk to one agent. It runs the whole crew behind the scenes — and hands you website copy, a social image, and announcement posts, each with an on-chain receipt.

04 · Two doors, one engine
DOOR A · HERO

Human web app

Next.js — intake, the live "Agent-Economy Theater", finished kit + provenance cards.

DOOR B · MANDATORY

Callable CAP service

Praeco listed on the CROO Agent Store — other agents (and humans) hire it over CAP.

Both doors funnel into the same CAP entry point — so every job, human or agent, is a real CAP order, and web buyers count toward the ≥5 unique buyer wallets.

07 · The competitive reality

The mechanic is taken. The vertical is open.

TAKEN — but thin

OPEN

The creator / launch vertical

"launch kit" → nothing. "marketing" → nothing. No one runs a creator product.

10 · Anti-sybil & cost

The moat is demand — cleared by design.

  • ≥3 unique counterparty agents — every run hires 3+ independent specialists (structural)
  • Zero self-trade — pure-composer never hires our own agents
  • ≥5 unique buyer wallets — RECTOR's audience, motivated by the kit + airdrop whitelist
